Player Controls and Actions

In OpenATBP, controlling your champion is simple but full of depth. Here is how to command the battlefield:

  • Left-click on the ground to move your champion to the clicked location.
  • Left-click on an enemy to begin attacking that target.
  • Right-click on the ground to send a ping to that location.

Champion Abilities

Each champion has three active abilities mapped to the Q, W, and E keys. These abilities can deal damage, apply debuffs, control enemies, or create helpful effects.

Each ability has its own cooldown. Once used, you need to wait before casting it again.

No Mana

OpenATBP does not use a mana system. You can cast abilities as long as they are off cooldown, letting you focus on timing, positioning, and decision-making.

Passive Powers

Every champion also has a passive ability. These are either always active or automatically trigger when specific conditions are met.

Damage and Death

Every unit, except Magic Man's decoy, has Health Points. When a unit reaches 0 health, it is knocked out.

Champion Knockouts and Respawning

When a champion is knocked out, they respawn at their team's base after a delay.

  • Base respawn time: 10 seconds
  • Respawn time increases by +1 second for every full minute the match has lasted.
  • Example: at 5 minutes, respawn time is 15 seconds.

Gaining Experience and Leveling Up

Defeating enemy champions, minions, and jungle creeps earns Experience Points. Earning enough XP causes your champion to level up.

Leveling Benefits

  • Your base stats increase.
  • You gain a Skill Point.
  • Skill Points can be invested into Battle Junk items from your backpack.

Battle Junk Upgrades

Battle Junk items can improve stats such as:

Offense

  • Attack Damage
  • Power Damage
  • Attack Speed
  • Critical Chance

Defense

  • Health
  • Armor
  • Shields
  • Regenerate

Utility

  • Attack Vamp
  • Power Vamp
  • Armor Breach
  • Shield Breach

Combat System

All units can deal damage, but not all damage works the same way.

Attack Damage

Attack Damage is physical damage used by basic attacks and some abilities. It is commonly used by champions, minions, jungle monsters, and towers.

Power Damage

Power Damage is magical or ability-based damage. Most champion abilities and jungle boss special attacks use Power Damage.

Disconnect Buffs

Disconnect buffs help balance uneven matches when teammates go offline. These buffs only apply in real matches, not PvP test matches.

If both teams have disconnected players, the buffs cancel each other out.

One Teammate Disconnected

  • +20% Armor
  • +20% Shields
  • +15% Movement Speed

Two Teammates Disconnected

  • +20% Attack Damage
  • +20% Power Damage

If all three team members disconnect, that team automatically forfeits the match.

Crowd Control Effects

Crowd Control, often shortened to CC, refers to effects that restrict movement, actions, or both.

Root

You cannot move or use movement abilities, but you can still attack and use non-movement abilities.

Stun

You cannot move, attack, or use abilities.

Charm

You lose control and automatically walk toward the charmer.

Slow

Your movement speed is reduced, but you can still attack and cast abilities.

Fear

You lose control and run in a random direction before freezing until the effect ends.

Silence

You cannot cast abilities, but you can still move and basic attack.

Blind

Your basic attacks deal no damage during the effect.

Polymorph

You are transformed into a Flambit and cannot use abilities or basic attacks.

Airborne

You are knocked back or pulled and cannot move or use abilities while airborne.

Altars

Altars are powerful control points that provide temporary team buffs.

Mid Altar

  • Located at the center of the map.
  • Grants +25% Attack Damage and Power Damage.
  • Usually the most important altar to contest.

Side Altars

  • Located on opposite sides of the map.
  • Grant +25% Armor and Shields.
  • Useful for surviving extended fights.

Capture Speed

  • If your team has a 1-player advantage on the altar, capture takes 5 seconds.
  • If your team has a 2-player or greater advantage, Fast Capture activates and capture takes 2 seconds.

Re-Capture

After an altar is captured, it becomes available again after 1.5 minutes.

Goo Buff Synergy

Players with the Goo Buff amplify altar effects by 50%:

  • Mid Altar becomes +37.5% Attack Damage and Power Damage.
  • Side Altars become +37.5% Armor and Shields.

Health Packs

Health Packs are pickups that provide instant healing and temporary regeneration.

Effects

  • Instantly restores 15% of your maximum health.
  • Grants +15 Health Regeneration for 1 minute.

Timing

  • Initial spawn: 14:00 into the match.
  • Respawn time: 60 seconds after being picked up.

Spawn Times and Respawn Times

Knowing spawn timers helps control the map and plan objectives.

Champions

  • Spawn at match start.
  • Respawn: 10 seconds + 1 second per match minute.

Minions

  • First wave: 14:30.
  • New wave every 30 seconds.

Small Jungle Monsters

  • Gnomes, Iron Owls, Grass Bear, and Hug Wolf.
  • Spawn: 14:15.
  • Respawn: 45 seconds after being killed.

Goo Monster

  • Spawn: 13:30.
  • Respawn: 90 seconds after being killed.

Kee-Oth

  • Spawn: 13:00.
  • Respawn: 120 seconds after being killed.

Health Packs

  • Spawn: 14:00.
  • Respawn: 60 seconds after being picked up.
